LevelEdge

×

LevelEdge was conceived through multiple phases of the UX design process - from discovery and ideation through to the various stages of mockup, prototyping and final design. I worked on the project during evenings and weekends across 5 months while managing a full-time job. LevelEdge allows for an efficient and trustworthy way to find and connect with music industry experts in order to quickly solve production issues.

Research

×

Based on my hypothesis I conducted competitive analysis and user interviews with both music professionals and amateurs. The few offerings available included pain points such as confusing messaging, dated propositions and convoluted pricing models. Through the interviews I uncovered several pain-points with services which were aimed at the same audience. These wranged from time consuming searches, lack of organisation to trust in the services and lack of credible experts.

To solve the highlighted issues I implemented the following solutions:

  • Rating and review system for trust and accountability
  • Category search and sort feature to quickly find relevant results
  • Communication feature which allows for pre-confirmation before from suitable expert
  • Notification facility to ensure no delays throughoput search, booking and communication process

Ideation

×

To help empathise with and keep the user at the forefront throughout the process I created 3 personas to represented potential users. I included background, behaviours, frustrations and goals for each persona.

Based on my personas and analysis I created 3 user flows which highlighted a range of features of LevelEdge; search, messaging and leaving a review. I identified what tasks needed to be performed to achieve each of the tasks.

Based on the user flows I created a sitemap illustrating the structure of LevelEdge. Through peer reviews and card sorting exercises I revised the sitemaps to make it the most logical and clear site structure possible.

Wireframing & Testing

×

Based on the user flows and sitemaps I drew up a series of low fidelity wireframes for both mobile and desktop. I used these to conduct a first round of testing. Issues which were analysed and resolved in revised mid-fidelity wireframes using Balsamiq. Some or those issues highlighted were:

  • A simpler filter page with reversed hierarchy
  • Menu repositioned for easier access on mobile devices
  • Added alerts confirming successful actions, access to cameraroll, etc.
  • Clarified the options on the landing page both visually and in copy
  • Made some entry fields optional

Finally, after further testing, I used Sketch to create a high fidelity version of LevelEdge which I used to prototype in InVision

Through out the wireframing process I conducted usability tests to fix errors and ensure that each iteration was improved. My focus remained on creating a friendly and trustworthy proposition while fixing smaller bugs and applying visual enhancements.

Design

×

Throughout the wireframing process the visual design of LevelEdge was developed to support the findings outlined above. I therefore created an interface using a bold colours and with engaging graphics to evoke a friendly and accessible user experience.
